About Me

Hey! I am Tanmay, and I work with Samsung Research Institute Bangalore as a researcher, in the Multimedia Division of the Advanced Technologies Lab. Currently, I am working on solving an interesting problem of swipe gesture recognition in the Samsung Keyboard and prior to this I was developing new algorithms for the keyboard. My previous work has been in the area of cognitive robotics, machine learning, computer vision and distributed computing. These projects have given me the opportunity to explore different fields in computer science and shape my research interests. I am curious to continue my research in theory and applications of machine learning and cognitive robotics.

I completed my Bachelors’ degree in Electronics & Instrumentation from BITS Pilani, Pilani in 2016. During my undergraduate years, I have worked in the Electronics Division of Bhabha Atomic Research Centre, Mumbai and also with Risk-Finance Technology Division in JP Morgan Chase, Bangalore. I was also awarded the DAAD-WISE Scholarship where I worked as a research intern with Dr. Sven Behnke and actively contributed towards developing vision algorithms for Humanoid Soccer bots.

Advanced Technology Lab - Multimedia Division

Developed a novel geometric algorithm for a virtual keyboard to predict a word corresponding to a swipe gesture input on matching a sequence of points to the potential candidate word path. Designed neural architecture that predicts the probable suggestions for the swipe gesture input by classifying it based on its shape. Implemented a real-time word completion framework for swipe gesture input by modeling a uni-directional GRU-RNN architecture with Connectionist Temporal Classification (CTC) as its loss function. Proposed framework resolves word using a weighted FST that achieves 90% accuracy in top-5 predictions and an average swipe saving time of around 0.65s.
